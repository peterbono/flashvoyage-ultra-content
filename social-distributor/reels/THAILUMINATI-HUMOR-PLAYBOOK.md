```

|  |     screen height)         |  |
|  |                            |  |
|  +----------------------------+  |
|                                  |  <-- Black padding (5-10%)
+----------------------------------+
```

**Layout rules**:
1. Black background (#000000) fills the entire 1080x1920 frame
2. Tweet header: profile pic (circle, ~50px) + name bold + @handle lighter, positioned top ~15%
3. Text: White, ~28-32px, regular weight (NOT uppercase), positioned below header
4. Video clip: Centered, width ~95% of frame, height ~45-55%, with slight letterboxing
5. NO gradient overlays, NO Montserrat, NO fancy design -- it's meant to look like a screenshot of a tweet with embedded video

**Text patterns** (opening hooks):
- "When you..." / "When the..." (60% of all meme reels)
- "Me in Thailand at [age]..." (10%)
- "My bro having..." (10%)
- "[Subject] at 3am after..." (10%)
- "How it feels when..." (10%)

**Text characteristics**:
- Sentence case (NOT UPPERCASE) -- this is crucial, it differentiates from the "motivational quote" style
- Short: 15-25 words max
- Always includes "Thailand" + Thai flag emoji
- Conversational tone, Gen Z slang ("bro", "homies", "vibe")
- Parenthetical kicker at end: "(worth it)", "(lol)", "(help)"

**Video clip sources**:
- Movie/TV clips: True Detective, Top Gun, South Park
- Viral clips: dancing baby on map, crying toddler, kids playing music
- Animal clips: lizard in mirror
- Office/workplace clips: people looking miserable at desks
- Duration: 5-15 seconds, usually just one clip looping or playing once

### HUMOR MECHANICS (What makes it funny)

**1. THE SUBVERSION**
Every single top-performing reel uses expectation subversion:
- Setup: serious/philosophical/emotional statement
- Punchline: it's just about Thailand
- Example: "You will meet a lot of people in your life... stay close to the ones who frequent Thailand"

**2. THE HYPERBOLE**
Everyday situations inflated to absurd proportions:
- Going to work = crying toddler being dragged
- Booking a trip with friends = Top Gun fighter jets
- Post-holiday blues = existential crisis at desk

**3. THE SHARED PAIN**
Humor rooted in universal travel experiences:
- Blowing your entire budget
- Post-trip depression
- FOMO when friends travel without you
- Not knowing what to do with your life at 29

**4. THE "TAG YOUR FRIEND" MECHANIC**
Every reel is implicitly or explicitly designed to be SENT to someone:
- "My bro having nightmares" = tag the bro
- "Homies at 3am" = tag the squad
- "Stay close to the ones who..." = tag people who love Thailand
- Captions always include "Send this to them" / "Tag them"

**5. THE IDENTITY SIGNAL**
The content works as identity reinforcement: "I am a person who loves Thailand." Sharing it says something about YOU, not just about the content. This is the key to virality.

## FLASHVOYAGE ADAPTATION STRATEGY

### What to COPY (directly applicable)

1. **The tweet-style meme layout**: Black bars, tweet header (use "Flash Voyage" + logo), text + embedded clip. This is 100% automatable.

2. **The "When you..." text formula**: Haiku can generate these from FlashVoyage articles. Example prompts:
   - From an article about Bali budget: "When you realise Bali costs less per day than your Uber Eats habit"
   - From an article about Bangkok street food: "Homies at 2am after discovering pad thai costs 1.50 EUR"
   - From an article about Chiang Mai temples: "Me explaining my 'spiritual journey' to my boss after 3 weeks in Chiang Mai"

3. **The faux-philosophical quote format**: Rewrite inspirational quotes with a travel twist. Fully automatable with Pexels stock + text overlay.

4. **Captions that trigger sharing**: Always end with "Envoie ca a un pote qui..." / "Tag ton pote qui..."

### What to ADAPT (FlashVoyage-specific)

1. **Language**: French (Thailuminati is English-only). Use the same casual tone but in French slang:
   - "Quand tu realises que..." instead of "When you realise..."
   - "Les mecs a 3h du mat apres avoir claque tout leur budget..."
   - "Moi en Thailande a 29 ans qui se demande quel metier faire..."

2. **Scope**: Thailuminati is Thailand-only. FlashVoyage covers ALL of SE Asia. Broader but same humor DNA:
   - "Quand tu decouvres que Bali coute moins cher que ta pause dej a Paris"
   - "Les potes quand t'annonces le trip au Vietnam"

3. **Editorial voice**: Thailuminati is pure bro-culture. FlashVoyage targets 24-35 unisex. Adjust:
   - Keep the humor but lose the "boys trip" gender exclusivity
   - Replace "bro/homies" with "pote/squad" (more inclusive in French)
   - Add couple/solo travel scenarios too, not just group trips

4. **Meme clips**: Thailuminati uses movie/TV clips (potential copyright issues at scale). FlashVoyage should:
   - Use royalty-free viral clips from Pexels/Pixabay
   - Use animated GIF-style visuals (3D baby on map = can be generated)
   - Use reaction clips that are clearly transformative/fair-use

### What to AVOID

1. **Don't copy the street interview format**: Requires being physically in Thailand with a camera crew. Not automatable.
2. **Don't copy the vlog montage format**: Requires real footage. The 58s "24 hours" reel got FEWER likes than 5s memes.
3. **Don't use copyrighted movie clips**: South Park, True Detective, Top Gun = copyright strikes at scale. Use stock or create original visual metaphors.
4. **Don't target only men**: Thailuminati's audience is ~80% male. FlashVoyage should be gender-neutral.

## SUMMARY: KEY TAKEAWAYS

1. **Thailuminati's #1 secret**: The content is IDENTITY content, not information. People share it because it says "I am someone who loves Thailand." FlashVoyage humor reels should make people feel part of the "SE Asia travel tribe."

2. **Effort is inversely correlated with consistency**: The 5-second meme clips are posted daily and maintain 100-400 likes each. The 60-second vlogs are rare. Volume beats polish.

3. **The tweet-style layout is the killer format**: Black background, tweet header, short text, embedded clip. It looks native to the platform and signals "meme" instantly. It does NOT look like a brand.

4. **Every reel must be shareable**: The test for any humor reel is "would I send this to a specific friend?" If the answer is no, kill it.

5. **French adaptation advantage**: There is essentially NO French-language account doing this for SE Asia travel. Thailuminati is English. VoyagesPirates does memes but broader. FlashVoyage can OWN this niche in French.

6. **Automation feasibility**: 90%+ automatable. Haiku generates the joke text, Pexels provides the background clip, ffmpeg composes the tweet-style layout. The only manual input is quality-checking the joke (is it actually funny?).
